Name Card Trick Art Methods

  • Lenticular Printing — plastic ridged overlay that shifts the image as you tilt the card. Can make the cylinder appear to rotate or pop out in 3D. Cost: ~$2-5/card for small runs.
  • Spot UV + Embossing combo — Matte card with glossy UV coating only on the cylinder shape + embossed (raised) cylinder outline. Creates a tactile 3D illusion. Much cheaper than lenticular (~$0.50-1/card).
  • Foil Stamping (holographic) — Holographic foil on the cylinder shape creates a shifting light effect that simulates transparency and depth. ~$0.80-1.50/card.
  • Transparent card — Print on clear/frosted plastic card stock. The cylinder is printed, rest is transparent. The card IS the cylinder. ~$1-2/card.
  • Anamorphic print — Distorted cylinder printed on the card that looks correct only when viewed from a specific angle (like street art illusions). Free to print, requires precise design work.
  • Die-cut — Card itself is cut into a cylinder/tube shape instead of rectangle. Memorable but hard to store in wallets.
