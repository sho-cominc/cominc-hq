## 0. STRATEGIC PREMISE

ComInc. is NOT a tour company posting pretty photos. It is a Cultural Translation company. Every piece of SNS content must reinforce this positioning: **Sho is the bridge between foreigners and local Japan.** The content should make people think "I need someone like this" — not just "I want to go there."

The kominka (130-year farmhouse) is the physical entry point. SNS is the digital entry point. Both lead to the same thing: trust, then services (tourism, relocation, real estate, business).

## 2. CONTENT PILLARS

Five pillars that map to ComInc.'s five service lines while feeling like authentic content, not advertising.

### Pillar 1: "Unseen Japan" (Discovery)
**Serves**: Tourists, Culture seekers
**Ratio**: 35% of content
- Seasonal landscapes nobody is posting (not Fushimi Inari)
- Local festivals, matsuri, hidden spots in Joetsu/Myoko
- Food that doesn't exist in Tokyo
- "I've lived here 5 years and just found this" moments

**Platform expression**:
- IG: Cinematic Reels (15-30s), carousel photo essays
- YT: "Hidden Japan" series (8-12 min)
- X: Photo + 1-2 sentence caption with a take ("Everyone goes to Hakuba. Nobody knows this place exists.")
- TikTok: Quick reveals, "POV: you found a village nobody knows about"
- Pinterest: Seasonal travel planning pins

### Pillar 2: "The Bridge" (Cultural Translation)
**Serves**: All audiences — this IS the brand
**Ratio**: 25% of content
- What gets lost in translation (literal and cultural)
- "What your Japanese host actually means when they say X"
- How Sho solves real problems for foreigners (anonymized stories)
- Bilingual content showing both worlds
- The gap between tourist Japan and real Japan

**Platform expression**:
- IG: Text-heavy carousels, talking-head Reels
- YT: Storytelling vlogs, "Day in the life of a cultural translator"
- X: Threads. This is thread gold. "5 things nobody tells you about living in rural Japan."
- LinkedIn: Professional angle — cross-cultural business challenges
- TikTok: Quick "Japanese culture explained" clips

### Pillar 3: "Powder & Peaks" (Ski/Outdoor)
**Serves**: Ski enthusiasts
**Ratio**: 20% of content (seasonal — 40% in winter, 10% in summer)
- Myoko powder days, conditions reports
- "Myoko vs Niseko" direct comparison content
- Backcountry, onsen after skiing, the full Myoko ski lifestyle
- Summer: hiking, mountain biking, green season appeal

**Platform expression**:
- IG: Action Reels, powder shots, before/after (empty slopes vs Niseko crowds)
- YT: "Full Day at Myoko" POV, gear guides, resort comparisons
- X: Real-time conditions, hot takes on ski culture
- TikTok: POV skiing clips, satisfying powder shots
- Pinterest: "Myoko ski trip planning" guides

### Pillar 4: "Kominka Life" (Stay & Relocate)
**Serves**: Relocators, Digital nomads, Culture seekers
**Ratio**: 15% of content
- The 130-year farmhouse: renovation, daily life, hosting stories
- What it actually costs/takes to live in rural Japan
- Visa, housing, community integration realities
- "A week at the kominka" guest experiences
- ES Hasegawa real estate partnership (subtle — "here's what's available")

**Platform expression**:
- IG: Aesthetic kominka interiors, seasonal changes, guest moments
- YT: Long-form relocation content, "Moving to rural Japan" series, house tours
- X: Hot takes on rural Japan living, cost of living threads
- LinkedIn: "Why remote workers are choosing rural Japan" articles
- Pinterest: Interior/lifestyle pins (kominka aesthetic has strong pin appeal)

### Pillar 5: "Community Intel" (Engagement & Utility)
**Serves**: All audiences
**Ratio**: 5% of content
- Polls, Q&A, "ask me anything about Myoko/Joetsu"
- Responding to comments with content
- Seasonal event calendars
- Practical info (transport, weather, what to pack)
- UGC reshares from guests

**Platform expression**:
- IG: Stories polls, Q&A stickers, community highlights
- X: Reply threads, community engagement
- YT: Community tab, comment responses in videos

## 5. CROSS-PLATFORM REPURPOSING STRATEGY

### The Content Waterfall

One piece of source content becomes 8-12 platform-specific pieces.

```
SOURCE: Sho shoots 3 min video at a hidden onsen after skiing

    YouTube (long-form)
    └── Full 10-min video: "After Skiing in Myoko: The Onsen Nobody Knows About"
        ├── Includes context, history, how to get there, cultural etiquette
        └── Timestamps, SEO description, end screen to next video

    Instagram Reel (30-60s)
    └── Best 30s clip with text overlay + trending audio
        └── Caption: storytelling + CTA to full YT video

    TikTok (15-30s)
    └── Vertical edit, faster pace, hook in first 2 seconds
        └── Different caption style (more casual, emoji-ok)

    X Thread
    └── "I just found the most underrated onsen in Japan. Thread:"
        └── 5-7 tweets: story format, photos embedded, link to YT at end

    Instagram Carousel
    └── 5-slide photo essay: the approach, the entrance, the bath, the view, the beer after
        └── Educational caption about onsen etiquette

    Pinterest Pins (x3)
    └── "Myoko Onsen Guide" / "Best Onsen After Skiing Japan" / "Hidden Hot Springs Niigata"
        └── Link to blog post or YT video

    LinkedIn Post
    └── "What a 400-year-old hot spring taught me about Japanese hospitality and business culture"
        └── Professional angle, subtle ComInc. mention

    Blog Post
    └── "The Best Onsen Near Myoko Ski Resorts: A Local's Guide"
        └── SEO-optimized, evergreen, links to all social content
```

### Repurposing Rules
1. **Never copy-paste across platforms.** Each platform has its own culture. Same story, different voice.
2. **YouTube is the source of truth.** Longest format goes there first. Everything else is derived.
3. **Instagram gets the best visual moment.** It's the highlight reel.
4. **X gets the best take/opinion.** It's the conversation starter.
5. **Pinterest gets the utility.** It's the planning tool.
6. **LinkedIn gets the professional insight.** It's the credibility builder.
7. **TikTok gets the hook.** First 2 seconds must stop the scroll.

## 7. BRAND VOICE PER PLATFORM

### Core Brand Voice (Universal)
- **Tone**: Warm, knowledgeable, slightly irreverent. Like a well-traveled friend who lives there.
- **NOT**: Corporate, overly polished, tourist-board generic, try-hard cool
- **Language**: English primary. Japanese sprinkled naturally (not forced). Bilingual captions where it adds value.
- **Perspective**: Always first-person ("I" or "we"), never third-person ("ComInc. offers...")

### Platform-Specific Voice Adaptation

**Instagram**
- Voice: Visual storyteller. Poetic but not pretentious.
- Captions: Medium length (150-300 words for carousels, 50-100 for Reels). Tell a micro-story.
- Example: "This is what 6AM looks like when nobody else bothered to wake up. Myoko, mid-February, -12C. The onsen steam rises into nothing. This is why I live here."
- Emoji: Minimal. One at most per caption. Never a wall of emojis.
- Hashtags: 10-15, mix of discovery (#JapanTravel) and niche (#MyokoKogen). In first comment, not caption.

**YouTube**
- Voice: Documentary narrator meets friend explaining over beers. Calm, informative, with personal asides.
- Thumbnails: Bold text, Sho's face, dramatic landscape. NOT clickbait — earned click.
- Titles: Search-optimized but personality-infused. "Myoko vs Niseko: Why I'd Never Go Back" > "Top 10 Ski Resorts in Japan"
- End screens: Always drive to next video or cominc.co

**X / Twitter**
- Voice: Sharpest version of the brand. Opinionated, concise, occasionally contrarian.
- Threads: Start with a hook that makes people stop. "Niseko is a scam. Here's why." Then deliver real value.
- Standalone tweets: One insight, one image. No fluff.
- Engagement: Reply to Japan travel, ski, and expat conversations. Add genuine value, not self-promotion.
- Example: "Everyone talks about Japanese hospitality. Nobody talks about the 47 unwritten rules you'll break in your first week here."

**TikTok**
- Voice: Fastest, most casual. Hook in 1.5 seconds or die.
- Style: POV, transitions, trending formats adapted to Japan content. Not forced trends — only when natural.
- Text on screen: Essential. Most watch without sound.
- Example hook: "POV: you found a ski resort with zero lift lines" [cut to empty Myoko powder run]

**Pinterest**
- Voice: Helpful, organized, aspirational. Think travel magazine editor.
- Pin descriptions: SEO-loaded. "Plan your Myoko ski trip with this local's guide to the best powder runs, onsen, and hidden restaurants in Niigata, Japan."
- Board names: "Myoko Ski Guide" / "Rural Japan Living" / "Joetsu Hidden Gems" / "Kominka Stay Inspiration"

**LinkedIn**
- Voice: Sho as professional cultural translator. Thoughtful, insight-driven.
- Content: Cross-cultural business observations, rural Japan economic opportunity, remote work from Japan.
- Example: "I translate culture for a living. Not languages — culture. Here's what most foreign businesses get wrong about rural Japan."
- Never: Tourist content. This is professional Sho, not guide Sho.

**Threads / Bluesky**
- Voice: Mirror X voice. Slightly more relaxed on Threads, slightly more intellectual on Bluesky.
- Effort: Minimal. Cross-post X content with light adaptation.
