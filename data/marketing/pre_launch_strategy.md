## 1.3 Tone of Voice and Copywriting Direction

**The voice**: Sho knows this place the way a good friend who moved back home knows it. Not a tour operator reading from a script. Not a government tourism board. A person who has lived internationally, came back, and genuinely wants to show you what he loves.

**Core principles**:
- Write in second person ("you") — keep it personal
- Short sentences. No fluff.
- Specific over generic: "13-16 meters of annual snowfall" beats "great snow"
- Specific over generic: "Kasugayama Castle, where Uesugi Kenshin built his base" beats "historic castle"
- Avoid tourism clichés: "hidden gem", "off the beaten path", "authentic experience" — show, don't label
- Use sensory detail: the steam of onsen, the weight of fresh powder, the smell of cedar in a kominka

**Voice examples**:

Generic (avoid): "Myoko offers world-class skiing and traditional Japanese culture."
ComInc. voice: "The lifts open at 8. By 8:15 you're in waist-deep powder that nobody else has touched. By noon you're in an onsen. That's Myoko."

Generic (avoid): "Experience authentic rural Japan."
ComInc. voice: "The farmhouse has been standing for 130 years. Four generations lived inside it. We're restoring it — and when it's ready, it's yours for a few nights."

## 2.5 Competitor Keyword Gaps to Exploit

Based on the competitor landscape (Section 3), these keyword gaps exist:

**Gap 1: Joetsu City content**
myokotourism.com and myoko.jp focus almost entirely on Myoko ski/nature. Joetsu City's history (Uesugi Kenshin, Takada Castle, sake, the Australian POW site) is barely covered in English. ComInc. can own this territory completely.

**Gap 2: Non-ski winter content**
Competitors are ski-first. Snowshoeing, kamakura building, onsen culture, winter food, snow photography — these are underserved in English content for this area.

**Gap 3: Year-round experience content**
Most English sites about Myoko go quiet in spring and summer. ComInc. can own the green season narrative: rice planting, hiking, river activities, harvest.

**Gap 4: The kominka story**
No competitor in this region is telling the kominka/farmhouse story in English. This is a completely open keyword category for Niigata/Joetsu.

**Gap 5: Personal guide positioning**
Search queries like "local guide Myoko Kogen English" or "private guide Joetsu Japan" return generic directories. A personal guide brand (Sho's name and face) can dominate these results because no one else is doing it.

## 2.6 Japan Travel SEO Landscape — What's Ranking and Why

**What ranks for Japan travel keywords:**
- japan-guide.com: Domain authority from 20+ years. Ranks for nearly every Japan destination keyword. Not beatable head-to-head — but can be beaten on long-tails and local depth.
- JNTO (japan.travel): Government site with massive DA. Ranks for destination overview terms. Zero personality — cannot rank for "which is better" or personal experience searches.
- Travel blogs (thewholeworldisaplayground, iamaileen, etc.): Rank for "personal experience" and "is X worth it" queries. These are ComInc.'s direct competitors for blog traffic — and ComInc. has an advantage because Sho actually lives here.
- Booking aggregators (Viator, GetYourGuide, Tripadvisor): Rank for transactional "tours in Myoko" terms. ComInc. should list on these as distribution, not compete against them for those keywords.

**Key insight**: The highest-converting SEO traffic for ComInc. comes from:
1. Long-tail informational queries that larger sites don't have specific content for
2. Personal, first-person content that review aggregators cannot produce
3. Comparison content ("Myoko vs X") that official tourism boards never write
4. Year-round content that ski-focused competitors don't publish in summer

# SECTION 3 — COMPETITOR LANDSCAPE

## 3.1 Primary Competitors — Myoko Ski / Snow Tourism (English-Language)

### myokotourism.com (Myoko Tourism)
**Type**: Official city tourism board English site
**Strengths**: Official status, comprehensive listings, good DA, listed by all major aggregators, covers all resorts and accommodation
**Weaknesses**: No personality, no Sho, generic tone, primarily ski-focused, does not cover Joetsu City at all, no blog/content marketing, no personal guide positioning
**Keyword territory they own**: "Myoko tourism", "Myoko accommodation", "Myoko ski resorts", "Myoko access"
**ComInc. opportunity**: Everything that requires a human voice — comparison content, personal recommendations, cultural depth, year-round green season content

### myoko.jp
**Type**: Independent destination information site
**Strengths**: Clean English site, well-structured, covers activities and access
**Weaknesses**: Thin on content, no e-commerce/booking, no blog, no personality
**ComInc. opportunity**: Depth of content on specific experiences — this site tells you what exists but not why you should care or which to choose

### Escape Myoko (escapemyoko.com)
**Type**: Ski school and guiding service
**Strengths**: English-speaking instructors, NZSIA certification, targeted at inbound skiers
**Weaknesses**: Ski-only (no cultural, no summer), no kominka angle, not positioned as a general inbound concierge
**ComInc. opportunity**: Year-round and cultural experience layer that Escape Myoko cannot offer; also the personal relationship angle vs. structured ski school format

### Myoko Snowsports (myokosnowsports.com)
**Type**: Official ski school at Lotte Arai Resort
**Strengths**: Institutional affiliation with Lotte Arai (luxury resort), English instruction
**Weaknesses**: Tied to one resort, no cultural layer, no guiding beyond ski slopes
**ComInc. opportunity**: Travelers who want something beyond the resort bubble

### Lotte Arai Resort
**Type**: Luxury hotel/ski resort (Korean-owned)
**Strengths**: Premium positioning, international marketing budget, Asia's longest zip line, strong English infrastructure
**Weaknesses**: Corporate, expensive, not "local Japan" — antithetical to what ComInc. offers
**ComInc. opportunity**: Travelers who are staying at Lotte Arai but want a real local experience outside the resort can be served by ComInc. as an add-on day experience

### Extremely Canadian (extremelycanadian.com)
**Type**: Canadian ski operator running tours to Myoko
**Strengths**: Strong brand in the backcountry ski market, operates group tours to Myoko from overseas
**Weaknesses**: Outbound operator, not locally based, ski-only, group format, expensive
**ComInc. opportunity**: The guests who want what Extremely Canadian offers but prefer private, locally-led, culturally integrated experience

**Type**: Cultural tour operator, Niigata/Yamagata

## 4.5 Reels Strategy

Reels receive 3-5x the organic reach of static posts on Instagram. Prioritize them.

**Reel formats that work for this context:**
- "POV: You're in Myoko" — first-person immersive footage
- "Things nobody tells you about [Myoko skiing / Joetsu / snow country]" — useful + scroll-stopping
- Before/after of kominka restoration (when available — massive viral potential)
- "I asked 10 travelers in Myoko why they came here" — vox pop style

## 6.2 Initial Paid Traffic Considerations

**General stance**: Do not run paid ads until the site has at least 2 weeks of organic traffic data. You need to know which pages are converting before spending money sending traffic to them.

**After 2 weeks, consider:**

**Google Ads — Search**
Target high-intent keywords with low organic competition:
- "guided tour Myoko Kogen"
- "Joetsu tour English"
- "kominka stay Niigata"
Budget starting point: ¥3,000–¥5,000/day. Narrow geo targeting not needed (targeting by keyword intent is sufficient). Use exact and phrase match. Track inquiries as conversions.

**Meta (Instagram) Ads**
Best use case: retargeting people who visited cominc.co but did not submit an inquiry. Install Meta Pixel on site at launch.
Cold audience ads: Less efficient for a new brand. Wait until you have 500+ Instagram followers and some UGC (user-generated content) before running cold traffic Meta ads.

**Google Ads — Display/Discovery**
Avoid initially. Not cost-effective for a small inbound tourism operation with limited budget.

**Budget recommendation for first 30 days**:
- Organic and community first — ¥0 ad spend for first 2 weeks
- Week 3–4: ¥50,000–¥100,000 on Google Search (highly targeted, high-intent keywords only)
- Meta retargeting: ¥20,000–¥30,000 (small spend, but retargeting is the highest ROI format)
