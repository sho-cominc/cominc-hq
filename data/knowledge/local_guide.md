# Local Knowledge Base — Joetsu / Myoko Area

## Overview
Joetsu City and Myoko City are located in the southwest of Niigata Prefecture, known as "Snow Country" (雪国). The area offers world-class powder snow in winter and lush green mountains in summer.

## Key Attractions

### Winter (Dec-Mar)
- **Myoko Kogen ski resorts**: Akakura Onsen, Akakura Kanko, Suginohara, Madarao, Tangram
- **Backcountry skiing**: Deep powder, guided tours available
- **Onsen**: Akakura Onsen, Tsubame Onsen (outdoor riverside bath)
- **Snow activities**: Snowshoeing, snow hiking, kamakura building

### Spring (Apr-May)
- **Takada Castle cherry blossoms**: One of Japan's top 3 night cherry blossom spots (4,000 trees)
- **Rice planting**: Experience traditional rice farming
- **Mountain wildflowers**: Myoko highlands

### Summer (Jun-Aug)
- **Hiking**: Mt. Myoko (2,454m), Mt. Hiuchi, Sasagamine highlands
- **Cycling**: Rice paddy routes, coastal routes
- **River activities**: Seki River kayaking
- **Festivals**: Joetsu Festival, Gion Festival

### Autumn (Sep-Nov)
- **Foliage**: Myoko highlands, Imori Pond (reflection spot)
- **Sake breweries**: Multiple local breweries open for tours
- **Harvest experiences**: Rice harvesting, vegetable picking
- **Soba making**: Local buckwheat noodle workshops

## Transportation
- **From Tokyo**: Joetsu Shinkansen to Joetsumyoko Station (~2 hours)
- **Local**: Car recommended. Rental available at station.
- **Airport**: Nearest is Niigata Airport (1.5 hours by car)

## Useful Notes
- Snow depth can exceed 2-3 meters in winter
- Many attractions are seasonal — verify operating dates
- English signage is limited — guests appreciate having a guide
