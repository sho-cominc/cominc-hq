## 1. Joetsu & Myoko Attractions by Season

### Winter (December - March)
**Ski Resorts - The Birthplace of Skiing in Japan**

Myoko Kogen is one of Japan's premier snow destinations, receiving approximately 14 metres of snowfall annually. The ski season typically runs from mid-December through early May.

| Resort | Highlights | Vertical | Lifts | Best For |
|--------|-----------|----------|-------|----------|
| **Akakura Onsen** | Historic resort (est. 1937), tree runs, onsen at base | ~700m | Multiple | Intermediate, families |
| **Akakura Kanko** | One of Japan's first ski resorts, well-groomed runs | ~700m | Multiple | All levels |
| **Myoko Suginohara** | Japan's longest run at 8.5km, steep bump runs | ~1,124m | Multiple | Advanced, long cruisers |
| **Seki Onsen** | Cult powder destination, 16m annual snowfall | 310m | 2 | Expert powder skiing |
| **Ikenotaira Onsen** | Family-friendly, gentle slopes | ~600m | Multiple | Beginners, families |
| **Lotte Arai Resort** | 951m vertical, 197ha skiable terrain, 14 courses, 12 off-piste zones, 15m+ annual snowfall | 951m | 5 (incl. gondola) | Advanced, powder, luxury |
| **Madarao Kogen** | Famous tree skiing, Madapow powder | ~600m | Multiple | Intermediate to advanced |

**Peak months:** January and February for best powder conditions; December and March can also deliver excellent snow.

**Other Winter Activities:**
- Snowshoeing and backcountry touring in Myoko highlands
- Winter onsen experience (rotemburo outdoor baths in snow)
- Takada Castle winter illumination
- Snow monkey viewing (day trip to Nagano possible)

---

### Spring (April - May)

**Takada Castle Cherry Blossom Festival (early to mid-April)**
- One of Japan's Top 3 Night Cherry Blossom viewing spots
- 4,000 cherry trees surrounding the castle moat
- Magical pink and purple night illuminations
- Takada Castle was built in 1610 by Tokugawa Ieyasu's sixth son, Matsudaira Tadateru
- Food stalls, hanami parties, and festive atmosphere

**Spring Skiing**
- Seki Onsen, Akakura Kanko, and Lotte Arai often remain open into early May
- Unique opportunity: cherry blossom viewing AND skiing on the same day

**Myoko Kan-bara Matsuri (May)**
- Held at Ikenotaira to celebrate the coming of spring
- Fireworks, lottery, traditional music and dancing, food stalls

---

### Summer (June - August)

**Takada Castle Lotus Festival (mid-July to mid-August)**
- 19-hectare outer moat covered in spectacular crimson and white lotus blooms
- One of Asia's most impressive floral displays
- Best viewing in early morning when flowers are freshly open

**Hiking & Trekking**
- **Mount Myoko (2,454m):** Tsubame Trail - steep and thrilling, chains near summit; June-November
- **Mount Hiuchi (2,462m):** Sasagamine Trail - well-maintained, Koya Pond and Tengu Garden en route; one of Japan's 100 Famous Mountains
- **Imori Pond Circuit:** 15-20 minute flat walk, barrier-free, mirror reflections of Mt. Myoko
- **Naena Falls:** Japan's Top 100 Waterfalls, 55m drop, nicknamed "Earthquake Waterfall"; 15-min walk from parking
- **Mt. Myoko Base Trek:** Akakura Onsen to Naena Falls via Shin-Akakura and Imori Pond; flat and suitable for all levels
- **Madarao Kogen Trek:** Selected among Japan's Best 100 Walking Routes; 50km total at ~1,000m altitude, multiple course options
- **Tsubame Onsen to Naena Falls Trail:** Forest therapy trail, part of Myoko-Togakushi Renzan National Park

**Beaches**
- Naoetsu and local beaches popular with visitors from Nagano and beyond

**Naoetsu Gion Matsuri (late July)**
- Over 300 years of history
- Mikoshi (portable shrine) processions, food stalls, fireworks
- July 29: floats parade through streets, rice barrel offerings to Yasaka Shrine

---

### Autumn (September - November)

**Imori Pond Autumn Foliage (mid to late October)**
- One of Myoko's most famous photo spots
- Trees surrounding the pond turn red, yellow, and orange
- Mt. Myoko reflection in still water creates stunning compositions

**Myoko Sky Cable**
- Gondola operates July through November
- Spectacular panoramic autumn color views

**Kenshin Festival (4th weekend of August)**
- Commemorates legendary warlord Uesugi Kenshin
- Parade of warriors in period armor
- Battle reenactment with rival Takeda Shingen's forces
- Held at Kasugayama, Joetsu

**Arai Festival (first weekend of August)**
- Japan's thickest, longest, and biggest straw rope carried through the city
- Traditional Araibayashi dancing, fireworks, sake

**Echigo-Kenshin SAKE Festival (annual)**
- Grand sake celebration in Joetsu City
- Features Niigata's renowned breweries plus wine and craft beer
- Over 40,000 visitors across two days

### Year-Round Attractions

**Sake Breweries**
Joetsu is home to 12 sake breweries across the region (3 in Joetsu City, 3 in Myoko City):
- **Katafune Sake Brewery** - Winner of International Wine Challenge 2015
- **Myokoshuzo Co., Ltd.** - Junmai Daiginjo production
- Multiple other local breweries offering tastings and tours
- Niigata is Japan's premier sake-producing prefecture thanks to pristine water and Koshihikari rice

**Craft Beer**
- **Otama Brewing** (Joetsu) - "World's Best IPA" at 2024 Beer Awards (Haneuma IPA)
- **Myoko Kogen Beer** - Brewed with pure Myoko highland mountain water and premium hops

**Local Cuisine**
- **Koshihikari rice** from the Kubiki area - considered Japan's finest
- **Kubiki oshi-sushi** - preservative-free pressed sushi
- **Kanzuri** - Myoko City's signature spicy chili seasoning (fermented in snow)
- **Noppe** - local stew dish, pairs perfectly with Joetsu sake
- **Nagashi Somen** at Naena Falls cafe - buckwheat noodles flowing on a water chute
- **Sasadango** - mochi wrapped in bamboo leaves
- Local soba and udon made with mountain spring water

**Onsen (Hot Springs)**

Myoko has 7 onsen areas with 5 different hot spring sources:

| Onsen | Established | Specialty | Notes |
|-------|------------|-----------|-------|
| **Akakura Onsen** | 1816 | Beautifying skin, wound healing | Largest in Myoko; "water for beautiful women" |
| **Tsubame Onsen** | Historic | Milky white water | 1,100m elevation; free outdoor baths (Ougon-no-Yu, Kawara-no-Yu) |
| **Seki Onsen** | 1729 | Traditional atmosphere | Oldest hot spring in Myoko |
| **Ikenotaira Onsen** | Historic | Black mineral-rich water | 24hr winter operation, recreation facilities |
| **Shin-Akakura Onsen** | Modern | Various mineral types | Convenient access |
| **Myoko Onsen** | Historic | Rejuvenating properties | Central location |
| **Suginosawa Onsen** | Historic | Mountain views | Peaceful setting |

**Tip for guides:** Rotemburo (outdoor baths) offer the ultimate experience - fresh air, mineral water, and mountain views. In winter, soaking in hot water while snow falls around you is a uniquely Japanese experience.

## 2. Inbound Tourism Guide Best Practices

### How Top Private Guides in Japan Operate

**Pre-Booking Phase:**
- Respond to inquiries within 2-4 hours (accounting for time zones)
- Ask detailed questions about interests, physical ability, dietary restrictions, group composition
- Provide 2-3 itinerary options at different price points
- Share sample photos and past guest testimonials
- Be transparent about what is and is not included in pricing

**Itinerary Design Principles:**
- Design around guest interests, not a fixed template
- Build in flexibility - have Plan B for weather changes
- Balance famous spots with hidden gems only locals know
- Include a cultural experience (sake tasting, temple visit, cooking class)
- Plan appropriate rest stops and meal timings
- Consider jet lag for newly arrived guests (gentler morning starts)
- Always have a wet-weather alternative plan

**During the Tour:**
- Arrive 10-15 minutes early at meeting points
- Carry a portable Wi-Fi device or ensure guests have connectivity
- Share historical and cultural context through storytelling, not lecturing
- Read the group's energy - adjust pace as needed
- Take photos of guests at key spots (they will love this)
- Have local recommendations ready for their remaining days
- Carry a small first-aid kit, business cards, and emergency contact list

**Post-Tour:**
- Send a thank-you message within 24 hours
- Share photos taken during the tour
- Provide a digital mini-guide for their remaining time
- Ask for a review on the booking platform
- Follow up after their trip with a brief message

### Key Differentiators for Rural Japan Guides
- **Deep local knowledge** that Google cannot replicate
- **Personal connections** with sake breweries, farmers, artisans
- **Seasonal expertise** - knowing the exact best viewing dates
- **Language bridge** - helping guests communicate with locals
- **Safety expertise** - especially for winter/mountain activities
- **Transportation navigation** - rural bus schedules, driving routes

**Hokuriku Shinkansen (Bullet Train)**
- Route: Tokyo Station --> Joetsu-Myoko Station
- Duration: Approximately 1 hour 50 minutes
- Departures: Platforms 20-23 at Tokyo Station
- Frequency: Multiple departures daily
- Cost: Approximately JPY 8,500 - 9,500 (reserved seat)

### From Narita Airport

**Option 1: Train (recommended)**
1. Narita Express (N'Ex) to Tokyo Station: ~53 minutes, departures every 30 min

**Option 2: Keisei Line**
1. Keisei Line to Keisei Ueno Station
2. Walk 7-9 minutes to JR Ueno Station

## 6. Safety and Logistics

### Weather Considerations

**Winter (December - March)**
- Heavy snowfall: Myoko receives 14-16m annually; among Japan's snowiest areas
- Temperatures: -5C to 5C at base; significantly colder at altitude
- Blizzard conditions can occur with limited visibility
- Road closures possible during heavy snow events
- Snow tires mandatory; chains recommended for mountain roads
- Dress in layers; waterproof outerwear essential

**Spring (April - May)**
- Variable weather; warm days but cold nights
- Late-season snowfall possible at altitude
- Cherry blossom timing varies year to year (typically early to mid-April for Takada)
- Rain more frequent toward May

**Summer (June - September)**
- Hot and humid at lower elevations (30-35C)
- Cooler in mountains (20-25C)
- Tsuyu (rainy season) in June-July
- Afternoon thunderstorms possible in mountains
- Typhoon season August-September
- Hydration and sun protection essential for hikers

**Autumn (October - November)**
- Comfortable temperatures (10-20C)
- Rain possible; layers recommended
- Early snow possible at high altitude from late October
- Best foliage: mid to late October around Imori Pond

### Avalanche Awareness

**Risk Level:** HIGH - Myoko is in one of Japan's heaviest snowfall zones

**Key Safety Points:**
- Never go off-piste without proper avalanche safety equipment
- Required gear: avalanche beacon (transceiver), probe, shovel
- Recommended: avalanche airbag pack
- Check daily avalanche bulletins from JAN (Japan Avalanche Network) at nadare.jp
- Observations taken at Myoko Suginohara ski patrol plot at Sasagamine
- Backcountry access requires submitting a trekking/ski itinerary
- Share itinerary with accommodation and emergency contacts

**Backcountry Protocol:**
1. Check JAN avalanche bulletin before departure
2. Submit itinerary at ski resort or online
3. Carry full avalanche safety kit
4. Never go alone
5. Inform accommodation of return time
6. If overdue, accommodation should contact police

| Service | Number | Notes |

| **Police** | **110** | English assistance available |
| **Fire / Ambulance** | **119** | Specify location clearly |

- Private rescue team fees are billed to the rescued party (can be expensive)

- Mobile reception can be limited in mountain areas; let someone know your plans

### Insurance Recommendations

**For Guests:**
- Strongly recommend travel insurance with:
  - Minimum JPY 5,000,000 (approx. $50,000) medical coverage
  - Mountain rescue / evacuation coverage
  - Adventure sports rider (for skiing, hiking, snowboarding)
  - Trip cancellation coverage (weather disruptions common in winter)

**For ComInc. (Guide Business):**
- Professional liability insurance (guide operations)
- General liability insurance (third-party injury/damage)
- Vehicle insurance if providing transport
- Workers' compensation if employing staff
- Event cancellation insurance for group bookings

## 7. Legal Requirements for Guide Operations

### National Government Licensed Guide Interpreter (通訳案内士)
- Certification administered by Japan Tourism Agency (JTA) under MLIT.
- Only certified individuals may legally use the title "通訳案内士" (tsuyaku annaishi).
- Exam pass rate under 10% — one of Japan's most challenging tourism qualifications.
- Since 2018 revision: Paid guiding services are legal WITHOUT this license. The license is no longer mandatory but remains a strong credibility signal.
- Certified guides command higher fees and gain preferred access with reputable travel agencies.

### Travel Agency Act Registration (旅行業法)
- Operating a travel business (organizing/selling tour packages) requires registration with the JTA.
- **Type 1:** Full-scope (overseas, inbound, domestic). Registered with JTA Commissioner. Guarantee: 70M JPY + share capital 30M JPY.
- **Type 2:** Inbound and customized tourism packages. Registered with prefectural governor. Guarantee: 11M JPY + share capital 7M JPY.
- **Type 3:** Inbound tourism within a limited region set by prefectural governor. Guarantee: 3M JPY + share capital 3M JPY.
- **Regional Operator:** Operator-organized tours limited to business office location and adjacent municipalities. Lowest capital requirements.
- **Cost reduction:** Joining an association of travel agents reduces required deposits to 1/5 of standard amount.
- All business establishments must have at least one certified travel supervisor (旅行業務取扱管理者).
- **For ComInc.:** Type 3 or Regional Operator license is likely sufficient for Joetsu-Myoko focused inbound tours.

### Practical Compliance
- Guides offering paid services should carry liability insurance even without formal licensing.
- Never present as "licensed" without holding the national certification.
- Keep records of all guest interactions and tours for regulatory compliance.

### Partnership Development Strategy
- **Mutual benefit:** Ensure partnerships are profitable for both sides. Offer consistent guest flow in exchange for priority booking, discounted rates, or commission.
- **Shared values:** Align with partners committed to quality guest experience and cultural authenticity.
- **Regular communication:** Monthly check-ins with key partners. Seasonal planning meetings before peak periods.
- **Quality control:** Periodically experience partner services firsthand. Gather and share guest feedback.
- **Exclusivity:** Negotiate exclusive experiences (private brewery tours, after-hours temple visits) to differentiate ComInc.

### Commission & Revenue Sharing Models
- Activity referral commission: 10-20% of activity price
- Accommodation referral: 5-15% depending on volume commitment
- Package deals: Negotiate net rates with partners, mark up for package pricing
- Restaurant partnerships: Often goodwill-based (send guests, they provide excellent service)
