## 1. JAPANESE MINPAKU REGULATIONS

### The Minpaku Law (住宅宿泊事業法 / Private Lodging Business Act)

#### Overview
- Enacted June 15, 2018 to legalize short-term rental of private homes to tourists.
- Commonly referred to as the "Minpaku Shinpo" (民泊新法) or "New Minpaku Law."
- Applies nationwide as the baseline regulation; local governments may impose stricter rules.

#### The 180-Day Annual Limit
- Maximum 180 nights per year that a minpaku property can be rented to guests.
- The "year" runs from April 1 (noon) to the following April 1 (noon).
- Counted by nights: one guest staying one night = 1 day of the 180.
- If a property needs to operate more than 180 days, it requires a hotel/ryokan license (旅館業法) instead.
- Tracking: Operators must maintain accurate records of all operating days.

#### Registration Requirements
- All minpaku must register with local government authorities as "residential lodging operators" (住宅宿泊事業者).
- Obtain a registration number (届出番号) — this must be displayed on all OTA listings.
- Submit notification (届出) to the prefectural governor or designated city mayor.
- Registration is free but requires documentation and compliance verification.

#### Required Documentation for Registration
1. Floor plan of the property showing room layout and measurements
2. Proof of ownership or permission from the property owner
3. Building certification / inspection records
4. Fire safety compliance documentation
5. Neighborhood notification proof (evidence you have informed neighbors)
6. Management plan (especially for owner-absent properties — see below)

#### Owner-Present vs. Owner-Absent (家主不在型)
- **Owner-present (家主居住型):** Host lives in or near the property and is available during guest stays. Simpler requirements.
- **Owner-absent (家主不在型):** Host is NOT present during guest stays. MUST appoint a registered housing management company (住宅宿泊管理業者) to handle operations. This is the model most relevant to ComInc.'s operations.
- Management companies must be registered with the MLIT and are responsible for guest safety, check-in procedures, neighbor relations, and regulatory compliance.

#### Bimonthly Reporting Requirements
- Operators must submit reports to the prefectural governor every two months.
- Report due dates: 15th of February, April, June, August, October, December.
- Content: Number of operating days in the past two months, number of guests hosted, breakdown of guests by nationality.
- Failure to report can result in penalties.

#### Penalties for Non-Compliance
- Operating without registration: Fines up to 1,000,000 JPY (1 million yen).
- Local governments actively conduct enforcement and on-site inspections.
- OTA platforms (Airbnb, Booking.com) are required to verify registration numbers and may delist non-compliant properties.

### Fire Safety Requirements (消防法関連)

#### Mandatory Equipment
- **Smoke detectors:** Required in all residential buildings since 2006. Must be installed in bedrooms and hallways.
- **Fire extinguishers:** Required for all guest accommodation properties.
- **Emergency lighting:** Required for owner-absent properties; must be installed along evacuation routes.
- **Escape route signage:** Exit signs and evacuation route markers required, especially for owner-absent type.
- Heat detectors recommended in addition to smoke detectors.

#### Foreign Language Requirements
- Evacuation route instructions must be available in foreign languages.
- Equipment usage instructions must be provided in foreign languages.
- Emergency phone numbers and transport information in foreign languages.
- Recommended: Multilingual emergency procedure cards posted near exits and in guest rooms.

#### Floor Area Classification
- If the total floor area of accommodation rooms is less than 50 square meters, the property falls under "general housing" classification under the Fire Service Act, with corresponding fire safety requirements.
- Properties exceeding 50 sqm face stricter fire safety requirements equivalent to commercial accommodation.

#### Fire Safety Inspection
- Properties must pass a fire safety inspection before registration.
- Common failure points: missing or expired fire extinguishers, non-functioning smoke detectors, inadequate emergency lighting, blocked evacuation routes.
- Annual reinspection recommended even if not mandatory in all jurisdictions.

### Niigata Prefecture Specific Rules

#### Local Ordinance Considerations
- Niigata Prefecture generally follows the national 180-day limit without additional day restrictions (unlike Tokyo's Shinjuku/Minato wards which limit to weekends only in some areas).
- Rural areas of Niigata are generally MORE welcoming of minpaku operations, especially in tourism-dependent communities like Myoko.
- Niigata has a Special Zone Tokku Minpaku (特区民泊) system used to promote farm-stay tourism in rural areas, though this is distinct from standard minpaku.
- Community notification requirements apply: neighbors must be informed of minpaku operations.

#### Myoko Area Considerations
- The Myoko area benefits from tourism-friendly local government policies.
- Ski season creates high demand (December-March) but the 180-day limit means careful calendar management is essential.
- Strategy: Prioritize high-value ski season nights (December-March = ~120 nights) plus cherry blossom (April), summer peak (July-August), and autumn foliage (October) to maximize revenue within the 180-day cap.
- If operating multiple properties, stagger operating periods to maximize total revenue across the portfolio.

### Alternative Licensing Options

#### Ryokan Business License (旅館業法)
- No day limit — can operate 365 days/year.
- Requires stricter building standards, more extensive fire safety measures, and health department approval.
- Front desk/reception requirement (can be relaxed with automated check-in in some jurisdictions).
- Higher ongoing compliance costs but unlimited operating days.
- Worth considering if a property consistently fills 180+ nights.

#### Special Zone Minpaku (特区民泊)
- Available only in designated National Strategic Special Zones.
- No 180-day limit within the special zone.
- Minimum stay requirement: typically 2-3 nights (varies by zone).
- Not currently widely available in Niigata/Myoko area.

## 2. VACATION RENTAL OPERATIONS

### Cleaning Management

#### Turnover Cleaning Standards
- **Timing:** Schedule cleaning immediately after checkout, with a minimum 3-hour buffer before next check-in.
- **Checklist approach:** Use a standardized cleaning checklist for every turnover. Include verification photos.
- **Key areas:** Bathroom sanitization, kitchen deep clean, linen replacement (all bed linens, towels, bath mats), floor cleaning, trash removal, amenity restocking.
- **Winter-specific (Myoko):** Boot/ski equipment drying area cleanup, entrance snow/mud management, heating system check, pipe check for freezing issues.
- **Summer-specific:** Dehumidifier check, mold inspection (bathrooms, closets), insect control, air conditioning filter check.

#### Cleaning Team Coordination
- Use property management software (PMS) that automatically triggers cleaning tasks upon guest checkout.
- Assign unique smart lock access codes for cleaning staff, valid only during their scheduled cleaning window.
- Require photo documentation of completed cleaning (bed made, kitchen clean, bathroom, common areas).
- Maintain a backup cleaning team for emergencies (cancellations, double bookings, extended guest stays).
- Budget: 5,000-15,000 JPY per turnover depending on property size and location.

#### Linen and Amenity Management
- Maintain at least 3 sets of linens per bed to allow for laundry rotation.
- Stock amenities for 2x expected maximum occupancy (shampoo, soap, toilet paper, kitchen basics).
- Use a tracking spreadsheet or PMS inventory module to monitor supply levels.
- Establish relationships with local laundry services for bulk linen processing.
- Japanese guest expectations: Yukata (cotton robes), slippers, and quality toiletries add perceived value.

### Key Exchange & Smart Lock Systems

#### Smart Lock Options for Japan
- **Popular brands:** SESAME (Candy House), Qrio, August, RemoteLock, igloohome.
- **SESAME:** Very popular in Japan, affordable (around 5,000-10,000 JPY), easy installation, API integration available.
- **Qrio:** Japanese brand, strong local support, works with existing deadbolts.
- **RemoteLock:** Cloud-managed, good for multi-property operations, integrates with major PMS platforms.
- **igloohome:** Offline-capable, generates time-limited PIN codes without internet dependency (useful for remote properties).

#### Best Practices
- Generate a unique access code per reservation, active only during the stay window plus a 30-minute buffer.
- Send access instructions in the pre-arrival message (2-3 days before check-in) and include in the digital house manual.
- Limit cleaning staff access to scheduled windows with role-based permissions.
- Maintain a physical key backup in a secured lockbox for emergencies — do NOT share lockbox location in regular communications.
- Test smart lock battery monthly; replace batteries proactively (every 6 months recommended).
- In heavy snow conditions: Ensure lock mechanism is protected from ice/moisture accumulation.

#### Check-In Automation Flow
1. Guest books via OTA or direct booking platform.
2. PMS sends automated confirmation email with basic info.
3. 3 days before arrival: Automated message with detailed access instructions, property address, parking info, Wi-Fi password.
4. Day of arrival: Smart lock code activated. Welcome message sent.
5. 30 minutes after scheduled check-in time: Automated "Did you arrive safely?" message.
6. Day before checkout: Reminder of checkout time and procedures.
7. After checkout: Smart lock code deactivated. Cleaning team notified. Review request sent (24-48 hours later).

### Guest Communication Automation

#### Essential Automated Messages
1. **Booking confirmation** (immediate)
2. **Pre-arrival details** (3 days before: access code, directions, parking, house rules)
3. **Day-of welcome** (check-in day: local tips, weather, emergency contacts)
4. **Mid-stay check-in** (for stays 3+ nights: "How is everything?")
5. **Pre-checkout reminder** (day before: checkout time, trash instructions, key return)
6. **Post-checkout thank you** (same day: thank you + review request link)
7. **Review follow-up** (3-5 days later if no review received)

#### Communication Channels
- **Airbnb:** Use Airbnb messaging (required for Airbnb bookings to maintain response rate metrics).
- **Booking.com:** Use Booking.com extranet messaging or Pulse app.
- **Direct bookings:** WhatsApp or LINE (LINE preferred for Japanese/Asian guests, WhatsApp for Western guests).
- **Unified inbox tools:** Hospitable, Guesty, Hostaway — aggregate messages across all platforms.

#### Response Time Targets
- Initial inquiry: Under 1 hour (critical for Airbnb algorithm ranking).
- During-stay issues: Under 30 minutes during business hours, under 2 hours after hours.
- Superhost requirement: 90%+ response rate within 24 hours.

## 3. OTA PLATFORM MANAGEMENT

### Airbnb Optimization

#### Listing Best Practices
- **Title:** Include location + key amenity + season appeal. Example: "Ski-in Myoko Retreat | Mountain Views & Private Onsen"
- **Photos:** Minimum 20 high-quality images. Lead with the best exterior/view shot. Use natural light. Include all rooms, bathroom, kitchen, view, unique features. Professional photography ($200-500) has proven 17.5% booking increase.
- **Description:** Front-load key selling points. Include relevant keywords naturally (Myoko, ski, powder, onsen, Niigata). Address common guest questions proactively.
- **Amenities:** Check every applicable amenity box — Airbnb search filters depend on this. Highlight: Wi-Fi, washer/dryer, kitchen, heating, ski equipment storage, parking, onsen/hot spring bath.
- **House rules:** Clear, firm but friendly. Cover shoes-off policy, quiet hours, trash separation, ski equipment storage, smoking policy.

#### Superhost Requirements
- 10+ completed trips in past 12 months
- 4.8+ overall rating
- 90%+ response rate
- Less than 1% cancellation rate
- Superhost status improves search ranking significantly

#### Airbnb Algorithm Factors
- Response speed (faster = better ranking)
- Booking acceptance rate
- Review quantity and quality
- Calendar accuracy (avoid cancellations)
- Pricing competitiveness relative to comparable listings
- Instant Book enabled (boosts ranking)
- Listing completeness (photos, amenities, description)

### Booking.com Optimization

#### Key Differences from Airbnb
- Commission-based model: Booking.com takes 15-18% commission (higher than Airbnb's 3% host fee).
- Stronger in European and Asian markets.
- Guests can book without an account — lower barrier to entry.
- No review-for-review system; guests review independently.
- Payment handled by Booking.com in most cases.

#### Optimization Tips
- Enable "Genius" program for higher visibility (offer discounted rates to Genius-level guests).
- Use the "Rate Plan" feature to offer multiple pricing tiers (non-refundable vs flexible).
- Upload maximum photos allowed. Booking.com favors listings with 20+ photos.
- Complete the "Facilities" section thoroughly — this drives filter-based search results.
- Reply to all guest reviews (positive and negative) on the platform.

### Vrbo (Vacation Rentals by Owner)

#### Market Position
- Stronger in the US/North American market.
- Family/group travel focused — properties with multiple bedrooms perform well.
- Commission: ~8% of booking total.
- Less popular in Japan compared to Airbnb/Booking.com but growing.

#### Optimization
- Highlight family-friendly features (high chairs, kids' equipment, safe spaces).
- Emphasize full-property rentals (Vrbo users expect entire homes, not shared spaces).
- Use the "Premier Host" program for better visibility.

### Multi-Platform Strategy
- List on all three major platforms (Airbnb, Booking.com, Vrbo) to maximize exposure.
- Use a channel manager (Beds24, Lodgify, Hostaway, Guesty) to synchronize calendars and prevent double bookings.
- Maintain consistent pricing across platforms (account for different commission structures in base pricing).
- Direct booking website: Offer 5-10% discount vs OTA prices to incentivize direct bookings (0% commission).

### Photography Best Practices
- Hire professional photographer: 20,000-50,000 JPY per property session.
- Shoot during best light conditions (golden hour for exterior; bright day for interior).
- Stage the property: Fresh flowers, neatly folded towels, lit fireplace/kotatsu, set dining table.
- Capture seasonal variations: Snow-covered exterior for winter listings, green/flower shots for summer.
- Include lifestyle shots: Coffee by the window with mountain view, ski equipment by the door, onsen steam.
- Minimum 20 photos; 30+ for larger properties.
- Update lead photo seasonally to match current conditions.

### Review Management
- Respond to EVERY review within 48 hours.
- Positive reviews: Thank specifically, mention a detail from their stay.
- Negative reviews: Acknowledge the issue, explain what has been fixed, invite them to return.
- Never argue or be defensive in public responses.
- For Airbnb: Write your review of the guest within the 14-day window (contributes to community trust).
- Target 4.8+ rating: This is the Superhost threshold and critical for search ranking.
- Proactively address potential issues mentioned in recent reviews before they recur.

#### Length of Stay Discounts
- 3-4 nights: 5% discount
- 5-6 nights: 10% discount
- 7+ nights: 15% discount
- 14+ nights (monthly stays): 25-30% discount

- **Last-minute discounts:** Reduce price 20-30% for bookings within 48 hours of check-in for unsold dates.
- **Far-out premium:** Maintain higher prices for bookings 6+ months in advance; reduce as dates approach if not booked.
- **Orphan day pricing:** If a 1-2 day gap appears between bookings, reduce price significantly to fill it.
- **Add-on services:** Offer paid extras — airport pickup, ski equipment rental, private chef, guided tours (referral to Kai/ComInc. guides), grocery pre-stocking.
- **Pet-friendly premium:** If property allows pets, charge 2,000-5,000 JPY per pet per night (growing market).

## 5. GUEST EXPERIENCE DESIGN

### Welcome Book / Digital Guidebook

#### Essential Sections
1. **Welcome message** — Warm, personal greeting. Brief intro to ComInc. and the property.
2. **Property guide** — Wi-Fi password, TV/entertainment, heating/cooling controls, kitchen appliances, laundry, bath/onsen usage.
3. **House rules** — Shoes off, quiet hours (10PM-8AM), trash separation guide (critical in Japan), smoking policy, ski/snowboard storage.
4. **Local area map** — Annotated with key locations: nearest convenience store (konbini), supermarket, restaurants, ski lifts, bus stops, onsen, ATMs.
5. **Restaurant recommendations** — 10-15 local restaurants categorized by cuisine type, price range, dietary accommodation. Include photos and walking/driving time.
6. **Activity recommendations** — Seasonal activities with booking info. Reference joetsuexplorer.com for guided tours.
7. **Transportation guide** — Local bus schedules, taxi numbers, shuttle services, driving directions to key attractions.
8. **Emergency procedures** — Police (110), Ambulance/Fire (119), nearest hospital, property manager contact, evacuation route.
9. **Cultural etiquette guide** — See below.
10. **Checkout procedures** — Checkout time, trash instructions, key/lock instructions, feedback request.

#### Cultural Etiquette Guide for Foreign Guests
- **Shoes:** Always remove at the entrance (genkan). Use provided slippers indoors; separate toilet slippers in bathroom.
- **Onsen/Bath etiquette:** Wash thoroughly before entering the bath. No swimwear. Small towel should not enter the water. Tattoo policies vary — private onsen avoids this issue.
- **Trash separation:** Japan requires meticulous trash sorting. Provide clear multilingual instructions with visual guides. Categories: Burnable (燃えるごみ), Non-burnable (燃えないごみ), PET bottles (ペットボトル), Cans (缶), Glass (ビン), Paper/Cardboard (紙/段ボール).
- **Noise:** Japanese homes have thin walls. Emphasize quiet hours and respect for neighbors.
- **Tipping:** Not customary in Japan. May be considered rude in some settings.
- **Chopstick etiquette:** Don't stick chopsticks upright in rice. Don't pass food chopstick-to-chopstick.
- **Greetings:** Basic Japanese: Konnichiwa (hello), Arigatou gozaimasu (thank you), Sumimasen (excuse me/sorry).

#### Digital vs. Print Format
- **Digital guidebook:** Use Touch Stay, Hostfully, or custom PDF. Easy to update, searchable, accessible on guest's phone. Include QR code for quick access. Can be updated in real-time.
- **Print version:** Keep a physical binder at the property. Some guests prefer tangible reference. Include laminated emergency/essential pages.
- **Recommendation:** Provide BOTH. Digital sent before arrival; print version at property.

## 6. PROPERTY MAINTENANCE

### Seasonal Considerations for Niigata/Myoko

#### Winter Maintenance (November - April)

**Snow Load Management:**
- Myoko receives 13-18 meters of annual snowfall. Accumulated snow on roofs can reach 2-5 meters.
- One cubic meter of snow weighs approximately 500kg — roof collapse is a real risk.
- Snow removal from roofs (雪下ろし / yukioroshi) may be needed 2-4 times per season depending on accumulation.
- Cost: Professional snow removal averages 15,000-30,000 JPY per session.
- Schedule proactive removal when accumulation exceeds safe load for the building type.
- Flat roofs require more frequent clearing than steep-pitched roofs.

**Yukigakoi (雪囲い / Snow Protection Barriers):**
- Install protective barriers around the building before winter (typically November).
- Protects from: snow pressure, sliding roof snow, wind-driven drifts.
- Protect ground-floor windows, garden features, and external equipment.
- Remove barriers in spring (April-May).

**Pipe Freezing Prevention:**
- Critical issue: Water pipes can freeze and burst during cold snaps.
- If property is unoccupied in winter: DRAIN ALL WATER from pipes every time you leave.
- Alternative: Keep heating running at minimum level (10-12 degrees C) — costs 15,000-30,000 JPY/month but prevents freezing.
- Inspect plumbing for signs of past freezing: patched pipes, water stains, buckled fittings.
- Insulate exposed pipes, especially those on exterior walls or in unheated spaces.
- Know the location of the main water shutoff valve and ensure guests/cleaners know it too.

**Heating Systems:**
- Ensure heating system is serviced before winter season (October-November).
- Stock kerosene for kerosene heaters (still common in rural Niigata properties).
- Maintain backup heating option (electric heater) in case primary system fails.
- Set minimum temperature for vacant periods to prevent pipe freeze and moisture problems.

**Entrance/Access:**
- Snow removal for entrance path, parking area, and emergency exits.
- Snow-melt sprinkler systems (融雪装置) are common in the area — maintain if installed.
- Provide guests with instructions for clearing entrance if heavy overnight snowfall.
- Stock snow shovel, broom, and de-icing materials at the property.
- Ensure drying space for wet ski gear, boots, and outerwear (essential amenity in ski properties).

#### Summer/Humidity Maintenance (June - September)

**Moisture and Mold Prevention:**
- Niigata has high humidity year-round (average 72% in winter, up to 71.4% in July).
- Rainy season (梅雨 / tsuyu) in June-July brings extended wet periods.
- Run dehumidifiers in all rooms, especially closets and bathrooms.
- Ensure adequate ventilation: open windows when weather permits, use exhaust fans.
- Inspect for mold monthly during humid months (focus on: bathroom ceiling, closet walls, under-sink areas, window frames).
- Use anti-mold products and moisture-absorbing packets in closets and storage areas.
- Ensure air conditioning systems are serviced and filters cleaned before summer.

**Pest Control:**
- Mountain/rural properties may attract insects (mosquitoes, mukade/centipedes, spiders).
- Stock insect repellent and provide mosquito coils (蚊取り線香) for guest use.
- Seal entry points around windows, doors, and foundations.
- Professional pest treatment recommended seasonally (spring and autumn).

#### Year-Round Maintenance

**Regular Inspection Schedule:**
- Monthly: Smart lock battery check, smoke detector test, fire extinguisher inspection, general property walk-through.
- Quarterly: Deep clean (professional), HVAC filter cleaning, plumbing inspection, appliance check.
- Biannually: External building inspection, roof condition assessment, gutter cleaning.
- Annually: Full property assessment, major appliance service, fire safety equipment certification, insurance review.

**Local Service Provider Network:**
- Maintain relationships with reliable local providers for:
  - Plumbing (especially emergency pipe repair in winter)
  - Electrical
  - Heating/HVAC service
  - Snow removal
  - General handyman/carpenter
  - Locksmith (smart lock issues)
  - Cleaning team(s)
  - Laundry service
  - Pest control
- Keep contact lists updated with after-hours emergency numbers.
- Budget: Reserve 5-10% of annual revenue for maintenance and repairs.

### Structural Considerations for Snow Country Properties

#### Building Features to Prioritize
- **Reinforced roof structure:** Houses in snow country need especially thick internal supporting posts.
- **Raised foundations:** Lifts the house above snow level, keeps ventilation and windows clear.
- **Steep roof pitch:** Allows snow to slide off naturally (reduces manual removal need).
- **Snow-melt systems:** Rooftop heating cables or ground-level sprinkler systems.
- **Double/triple glazed windows:** Essential for insulation in severe cold.
- **Proper insulation:** Wall, floor, and ceiling insulation significantly reduces heating costs.
- **Boot room/mud room (玄関):** Spacious entrance area for removing and drying wet gear. Critical for ski properties.

#### Pre-Purchase/Renovation Checklist for Myoko Properties
- Check roof load capacity documentation.
- Verify snow removal access (can equipment reach the roof?).
- Inspect foundation for settling or water damage.
- Test all plumbing under pressure.
- Verify insulation levels (older Japanese homes may have minimal insulation).
- Check heating system capacity for the property size.
- Assess parking access in heavy snow conditions.
- Confirm property is within snowplow route for road access.
